This thesis collates and evaluates the text of the Gospel of John as it appears in the writings of Gregory of Nyssa. For each verse Gregory cites, the apparatus registers the citations, references, and adaptations across his corpus — *Contra Eunomium*, the homilies on Canticles and Ecclesiastes, *De vita Moysis*, and the rest — with their locations in the Gregorii Nysseni Opera (GNO) edition, and weighs what Gregory's text contributes as patristic evidence for the Johannine text.

The John 1 apparatus included in this sample carries two of the study's text-critical observations at John 1:3–4: the evidence that Gregory's text read οὐδέν rather than οὐδὲ ἕν at v. 3, and the evidence — which the thesis judges overwhelming — that Gregory punctuated with a full stop after ἐν αὐτῷ (ὃ γέγονεν ἐν αὐτῷ. ζωὴ ἦν…), a punctuation the thesis finds unique among the New Testament manuscripts and the Fathers, reflecting Gregory's understanding of John 1:4 as giving the title "Life" to Jesus.
