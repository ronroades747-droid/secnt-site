The Prologue's architecture rests on two mechanisms, and they have been established in turn: the [foyer-bracket](/commentary/john/1-1-to-3/02-foyer-bracket/), the shape of the unit in space, and the [macro-tense spine](/commentary/john/1-1-to-3/03-macro-tense-spine/), its movement in time. They are not rival accounts of the Prologue's structure, one of which the reader must choose between. They are two real features of a single composition, operating at once and on the same words. The foyer-bracket is the Prologue's architecture in space — the arrangement of its parts, the opening and the closing framing the middle. The macro-tense spine is its architecture in time — the patterned movement of being into becoming that runs the unit's length. A composition can be shaped and moved at the same time, and this one is. To articulate the architecture fully is to hold both together.

## Where the two mechanisms meet

The two mechanisms are not merely parallel; they share their termini. The opening of the unit is at once the foyer pole of the v. 1 / v. 18 bracket and the point at which the spine's eternal-being register is laid down: the πρὸς τὸν θεόν that the closure answers is stated in the same clauses where ἦν stands. And v. 3 is the sharpest point of intersection. The creational statement πάντα δι' αὐτοῦ ἐγένετο is simultaneously the foyer pole of the v. 3 / v. 18 creator-and-exegete bracket and the hinge at which the spine turns from ἦν to ἐγένετο. The verse that frames the Gospel's redemptive narrative as the work of its Creator is the same verse at which the grammar pivots from the Word who was to the all that came to be. The shape and the movement are fastened to the same words. This is what it means to call the architecture deliberate: a single compositional decision is doing two kinds of structural work at once.[^coordinate]

## The asymmetry

Coordinate in operation, the two mechanisms are not coordinate in confidence — and the difference is not a loose end to be tidied but a precise statement of where the architectural argument stands. The diachronic spine is held at very-high confidence at both the registers on which it operates: that the pattern functions across the unit, and that it functions as the evangelist's design. Its evidence base is complete, because the pattern is verifiable on the bare distribution of the tenses across a Prologue we possess in full; nothing further is awaited. The synchronic foyer-bracket is held at very-high confidence at its pair-instances — the v. 1 / v. 18 and v. 3 / v. 18 brackets are established as deliberate pairings — but only at high and provisional confidence at the Prologue-wide pattern, because the unit-wide claim requires that all its bracket-pairs operate at the compositional register, and one pair, the life-and-light of v. 4 answered by the δόξα of v. 14, has not yet been engaged there. The asymmetry tracks the completeness of the evidence, not the strength of the patterns where each is established. To state both arms at full force would overclaim the bracket; to hold both as provisional would underclaim the spine. The architecture is stated honestly only when the asymmetry is stated with it.

## Reinforcement is not discharge

It is worth being exact about what the spine's full lock does and does not do for the bracket. The spine's being established at both registers reinforces the joint reading: it is a second, independent structural mechanism pointing to the same conclusion — that the Prologue is a composition of deliberate design rather than an accretion. But reinforcement is not discharge. The spine's completeness cannot complete the bracket's unit-wide claim, because the two mechanisms rest on different evidence, and the bracket's missing pair is its own to supply. The spine stands beside the bracket; it does not stand in for the pair the bracket still owes.

## The register of the coordinate claim

Two things must therefore be said at two registers. That the Prologue is shaped and moved at once — that the synchronic and diachronic mechanisms are coordinate and meet at shared termini — is settled, and is stated here at full force, because it is a claim about resolutions that are themselves fixed. But the unified claim that the Prologue is a completed architectural whole at both axes inherits the weaker arm's ceiling: it stands at high and provisional confidence, the spine contributing its very-high and the bracket its high-pending-discharge. The single condition under which the unified claim rises to very-high at both axes is the engagement of the v. 4 / v. 14 pair at the deliberate-compositional register, reserved for the treatment of 1:14; the spine arm requires nothing further. When that pair is discharged, the coordinate architecture closes; until then it stands strongly supported, with the one outstanding pair named.[^register]

[^coordinate]: The shared termini draw on resolutions established elsewhere in the unit: the personal-communion sense of πρὸς τὸν θεόν and the universal-scope, absolute-foreclosure sense of πάντα δι' αὐτοῦ ἐγένετο at the foyer end, and the deliberate pairing of each opening term with its answering term at the closure (v. 18). The bracket-pairs are set out in the treatment of the [foyer-bracket pattern](/commentary/john/1-1-to-3/02-foyer-bracket/); the three-term tense movement and its convergence across independent commentary traditions in the treatment of the [macro-tense spine](/commentary/john/1-1-to-3/03-macro-tense-spine/).

[^register]: The third bracket-pair — the life-and-light of v. 4 answered by the δόξα of v. 14 — has its opening terminus in 1:4–5 and is engaged at the deliberate-compositional register in the treatment of 1:14. Until then the unit-wide framing claim is held as strongly supported but not closed; the diachronic spine, whose evidence rests on the full distribution of the tenses across the Prologue, requires no such further engagement.
