Among the four Gospels, John is the one that reaches furthest back and furthest down. It opens not with a manger or a genealogy but before creation — "In the beginning was the Word" — and it carries the simplest vocabulary in the New Testament alongside its most searching thought. Its surface is plain enough to read aloud to a child; its depths have held the attention of the church's most careful readers for twenty centuries and are not yet exhausted.

This volume takes up the Prologue — the first eighteen verses — and works through it verse by verse, for those who teach and preach it. John's overture is a complete composition in its own right, a single arc from the Word with God to the Word made flesh that holds in miniature nearly every theme the Gospel will unfold. The aim is the scholarship that stands beneath a sermon rather than the sermon itself: the questions of text, language, and meaning worked out carefully enough that a teacher can build on the result and a reader can see how it was reached. It is written for ministers, seminary students, and teachers — serious about the details, and clear about why they matter.
