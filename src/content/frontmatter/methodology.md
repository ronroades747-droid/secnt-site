# Methodology

This page is the means-of-production counterpart to `/architecture-principles/`. Where that page articulates *what* the architectural-systematic shape is, this page articulates *how* that shape is produced — the editorial model under which the scholarship is adjudicated, the depth and reach of the research behind each position, the source-engagement standards, the shift from working notation to published apparatus, the calibration of confidence to the scope of a claim, the governance under which locked positions are refined, and the design of the whole to be carried forward by editors who come later. Several disciplines operate at both registers and are engaged at both pages; cross-references hold the coordination without re-argument.

SECNT scholarship is produced in an assisted editorial mode. A Chief Editor exercises adjudicative authority — engaging the sources, weighing the readings, pushing back on weak ones, and locking a position — over drafting and source-engagement that are AI-assisted. The assistance generates, surfaces, and presses; it does not adjudicate. Every locked position, every confidence assignment, and every "what could shift this" rests with the editor, who is accountable for the result. The standards this page sets out are the standards the editorial role is held to, and they are written to be transferable: as the series is accepted and others come to help, they come in an editor's role, trained into the same standards.

This means of production is disclosed here plainly rather than left to inference. The scale of the work may suggest a standing staff; the accurate account is a seasoned editor working in a disclosed assisted mode under named standards, which is what allows one editor to work across the registers the series spans. And it is not a departure from scholarly practice. Across multiple disciplines, discovery and presentation are already trending toward computational assistance operating under human accountability — a convergence the scholarly community is actively codifying as *disclosed, human-accountable* use rather than unguarded automation. Scholarly-publishing governance has settled on the principle that AI tools may assist but cannot be authors, because they cannot bear accountability, and that human authors remain accountable and must disclose their use.[^1] The editor's own field already works this way: the computational Coherence-Based Genealogical Method behind the standard critical Greek text assists the scholar's judgment without replacing it.[^2] The same convergence has been recognized at the highest level of the sciences.[^3] SECNT instances this convergence at primary-text depth, fully disclosed, under a named editor.

## §1 How a passage is worked through

Behind every position the Commentary states lies research carried to the point where the passage has nothing further to yield. The work proceeds one question at a time. Each interpretive question a passage raises is taken up on its own and worked in depth — what a tense carries, what a word means here, which reading the manuscript tradition supports, how a clause bears on the one beside it. That is the topical work. But the questions in a passage are not independent, and the harder labor is cross-topical: how the resolution of one question constrains another, how a decision about a single word reaches forward into the shape of a paragraph, how the parts cohere into the whole. The research does not stop at the first defensible answer to each question taken in isolation; it continues until the questions have been worked against one another and the passage is, in the proper sense, exhausted.

The questions are not all of one kind, and the work takes the shape each calls for. Some resolve the sense of a single grammatical or lexical point. Some resolve which reading the edition adopts where the witnesses divide. Some resolve how several clauses cohere as one argument rather than a sequence of separate remarks. And some resolve a pattern spanning a whole unit — a bracket, a progression, a deliberate shape across many verses — which rests on the smaller resolutions beneath it. Knowing the kind of question before answering it keeps the standard of evidence matched to the claim: a pattern asserted across a whole unit is held to a higher bar than the sense of a single word.

Only when a question has been worked to that depth is a position fixed — stated with an explicit confidence and an account of what would change it. The reader is therefore meeting not an opinion but the settled result of research that went as far as the passage allows. That is the ground of the Commentary's credibility: not authority asserted, but the work behind each position, done to exhaustion and left open to inspection.[^4]

## §2 Source-engagement as editorial standard

Source-engagement is where editorial judgment meets the scholarly tradition, and it is governed by a standard rather than left to the editor's discretion. The standard fixes how sources are read, at what depth, and how their bearing on a position is disclosed. Four commitments carry it.

**Engagement-layer disclosure.** Every source is named at the layer at which it is actually brought to bear. There is a real difference between a position grounded in the editor's direct engagement with a primary text at the point that matters, one resting on a standard reference work, one relying on an author's own summary of a view rather than the underlying argument, and one carried only through another scholar's citation. SECNT discloses which of these is the case for each source, so that a reader weighs not only the position but the basis on which it stands. Most commentary cites; it does not ordinarily disclose the depth of its own engagement. The standard here is that it must.[^5]

**Primary text before citation-chain.** Where a source bears on a position and the editor can reach the source itself, the standard requires engaging it directly rather than through another scholar's report of it. Citation-chains compound small errors and flatten the nuance that matters most; a commentator read at first hand often proves to converge with, or diverge from, a position in ways a secondhand summary conceals — a writer may grant a point in framing while reading the substance another way entirely, and only direct engagement catches it. The commitment is named precisely because it is costly and easy to shortcut: it is an editorial standard, not a private preference, and every editor is held to it.

**Convergence as the measure of confidence.** The confidence a locked position carries is tied to the breadth and independence of what stands behind it. A reading on which several independent scholarly traditions converge — the front-rank sources a serious position must reckon with — can be held at high confidence; a reading resting on a single tradition cannot, however distinguished that tradition is. And the bar rises with the reach of the claim: a pattern asserted across a whole compositional unit is held to a higher convergence requirement than the sense of a single word, because more is being claimed.[^6]

**The editor's own primary research, where it bears.** Where the editor has done original primary-text work bearing on a reading the published literature also treats, the original work takes precedence at the specific points where it is load-bearing. For the present volume, this includes original collation of a Greek Father's text of John; at the loci where that collation bears on a reading, it takes precedence over the published secondary treatment of the same witness. The principle is bounded: it operates only where the editor's contribution is genuinely original primary-text work on material the published literature also treats, and nowhere else does the secondary literature lose its normal authority.[^7]

These four are the standard, not the present editor's habits — which is why they are written down. They make each position evaluable on its basis, and they are what an editor brought into the series is trained into and held to. The work is meant to earn trust through how its sources are handled — visibly, at depth, and on the record.

## §3 The apparatus-shift: from working record to published footnote

The adjudication is recorded in one register and read in another, and the two are deliberately not the same. The working record carries the notation an editorial process needs and a reader does not: identifiers that locate each adjudication within the passage, the coded form in which each source's engagement-layer is tagged, the connections that tie one adjudication to the others it depends on, and the working entries by which the series keeps account of how its positions were made. This notation is load-bearing for the editor and for the editors who follow, because it is what keeps the adjudication auditable and the method self-documenting. It is not what the reader meets.

When a locked position is rendered into the published Commentary, the apparatus shifts register. What crosses over is the substance of the adjudication: the sources actually engaged, the readings weighed, the consideration that proved decisive, the confidence the position carries and what would shift it, and — preserved through the shift — the layer at which each source was engaged, so the reader is still told whether a position rests on direct engagement with a primary text or on a secondhand report of it. What stays behind is the scaffolding: the identifiers, the coded notation, the internal connections — the apparatus of making, not of reading.

The result is apparatus that is substantial without being cluttered. Substantial, because every published note renders a real adjudication rather than being composed for display; uncluttered, because the reader is given the scholarship and not the plumbing. The shift is governed by one test rather than left to taste: does this serve a reader's ability to weigh and follow the position? What passes crosses over as a footnote; what serves only the making of the position stays in the working record.[^8]

This is what stands behind the apparatus the published Commentary carries, and why it can be at once fuller than a reader expects and free of the machinery that produced it.[^9]

## §4 Two claims, each at its own confidence

A single piece of research often bears on two claims at once, and they are not equally secure. There is the near claim — what this word means here, what this clause does, what this pattern is at this point in the text — and there is the far claim it may serve: that the same feature contributes to something running across the whole Gospel, a way the book is built or a thread it sustains from end to end. The research that settles the near claim also begins to bear on the far one, but it does not settle it.

So the two are locked separately, each at the confidence its own evidence warrants, and the difference is stated rather than blurred. Where the sources converge, the near claim can be held firmly — at high or very high confidence, on the strength of independent traditions agreeing. The far claim, resting as it does on more than one passage, is held only as far as a single passage can carry it: registered as genuinely grounded, but provisionally, pending the wider work — the reading of the rest of the book, or a dedicated study — that alone could raise it. A firm result on the near claim is never allowed to lend its confidence to the far one. The reader is told which is which.

This is a discipline against the most natural form of overreach: letting a striking local finding swell into a sweeping thesis it cannot yet support. The temptation is real precisely because the near result is often strong, and a strong result invites the larger generalization. The standard holds the two apart on purpose. It is also why a reader will sometimes find a position stated with full confidence at the verse and, alongside it, a larger claim it feeds carried only as a possibility the series is building toward — not hedging, but honesty about how far the evidence has actually reached.[^10]

## §5 Settled, not closed

A locked position is settled, but it is not closed. The discipline that governs it afterward holds two things at once: it will not be re-argued on the evidence already weighed, and it will be reopened when something genuinely new bears on it.

The first half guards against churn. Once a question has been worked to exhaustion and a position fixed, the same evidence is not relitigated; a settled result stays settled until there is a reason beyond restlessness to disturb it. The second half guards against dogmatism. A locked position is refined — deepened, sharpened, sometimes recalibrated in confidence — when new substantive evidence appears, when the method itself develops, when teaching or production surfaces something the research had not, or when other scholars respond. Most often this deepens a position rather than overturning it; outright replacement happens only when the conditions named at the time of the lock — the account of what would change it, set down with the position itself — are actually met. The earlier work is built upon, not discarded, and the threshold for discarding it was fixed in advance, in the open. It is also how a claim held only provisionally comes to be raised: not by fiat, but when the wider work that alone could warrant it has been done.

There is a second, slower form of change, which reaches not the position but the standard it was held to. The standard the work measures itself against is not treated as final. Later work may reach a depth, or notice a register of the text, that the present standard does not yet articulate; when it does, earlier positions can be revisited at the higher standard rather than left at the one available when they were fixed. The series acknowledges this at every lock — that a better standard may one day bear on the work — without pretending to know now what that standard will be. The commitment is not that the work is finished, but that it is made to be brought forward.

Taken together, these are what make the scholarship both durable and alive: positions stable enough to be relied on, and a standard honest enough to keep rising. The work is not claimed to be complete; it is built to be carried forward — which raises the question of who carries it.[^11]

## §6 Built to be carried forward

Everything set out above — research carried to exhaustion, sources named at the depth they were engaged, confidence stated and held apart by scope, positions refined under genuine new input against a standard allowed to rise — is the series' editorial standard, not one editor's private practice. That distinction is deliberate, and it is the reason the standard is written down at all. A practice lives and dies with the person who keeps it; a standard can be taught, held to, and inherited.

At present the work is carried by a single editor, able to work across its registers — the research, the writing, the teaching, the building of the platform — because of long experience and the assistance described at the head of this page, which amplifies seasoned judgment rather than supplying it. That breadth in one person is exceptional, and the series does not assume it will recur. It assumes the opposite: that the work is larger than any one editor's reach, and that as the series is received and others come to help, they come in an editor's role, in the same assisted mode, held to the same standards.

What every editor shares is the standard — the disciplines of the preceding sections, which apply to the writing, the teaching, and the platform alike. What differs from one editor to the next is the competence a particular kind of work requires: the languages and exegetical judgment the research demands, the pedagogical judgment the teaching demands, the design and technical judgment the platform demands. The standard is what an incoming editor is trained into and accountable to; the competence is what qualifies that editor for the work in hand. This is how the series can grow without diluting — the bar stays uniform even as the hands multiply.

That is the final sense in which the series is built to last. Not that its conclusions are fixed — they refine, and the standard above them rises. Not that it rests on one editor — it is made to be carried by editors who come later, and the standards are written down precisely so that it can be. A commentary built this way is meant to outlive the hands that began it, and to be better in the hands that follow.[^12]
