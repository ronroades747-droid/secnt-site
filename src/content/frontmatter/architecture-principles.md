# Architecture Principles

SECNT is an architectural-systematic commentary series at the seminary-pastoral register. The series's distinctive shape — what makes it architectural-systematic rather than exegetical, pastoral, grammatical, or critical-historical — rests on four architectural principles that govern how scholarly work becomes published content, how that content is rendered for reading, how it is stratified across audiences, and how it integrates as a coherent whole. This page articulates the four principles substantively and articulates the architectural-systematic commentary genre the four together constitute.

The four principles are not independent stipulations. Each grounds, operationalizes, or operates within the others. The Brief–Commentary identity discipline names what the published artifact is. The audience-stratified dual-artifact landscape names who the artifact serves. The apparatus-substantial Commentary / pedagogically-scaffolded Lecture discipline names how the audience architecture operationalizes against the substantive scholarly work. The reader-facing architectural diagrams discipline names how visual content operates within the published Commentary at substantive joints. The four together produce the architectural-systematic commentary genre articulated at the closing synthesis below.

The page operates as the architectural counterpart to Methodology. Where Methodology articulates how the architectural shape is produced — the matured ARB workflow, the four-type ARB taxonomy, the source-engagement disciplines, the standing governance regimes — this page articulates what the architectural shape is. Several patterns operate at both registers and are engaged at both pages; cross-references hold the coordination without re-articulation.

## §1 The Brief–Commentary identity discipline

The Brief and the published Commentary are the same artifact at different registers. The Brief is the scholarly manuscript; the published Commentary is the manuscript rendered for web reading. The Brief's substantive sub-section structure is the page tree's directory structure; each substantive sub-section publishes as one page with one paired Lecture. The count of pages and Lectures per cycle follows from the Brief's substantive structure rather than being targeted in advance.

What this means operationally: what is in the substantive artifact is what is published. The Brief's body prose carries the substantive argument that the Commentary publishes. The Brief's footnotes carry the apparatus that the Commentary publishes as footnotes. The Brief's sub-section structure is the Commentary's page tree. There is no transformation between manuscript and publication beyond rendering for the medium — the substantive content does not change registers when it moves from manuscript to web.

This discipline differs from how surrounding evangelical commentary series handle the research-to-publication transition. Most series treat research and publication as discontinuous artifact-stages: a research apparatus accumulates during the writing of the commentary and is partially shed at publication, with the published Commentary carrying the conclusions of the research apparatus rather than the apparatus itself.[^1] The BECNT series structures volumes as a research-anchored Introduction followed by a published Commentary, with the Introduction articulating the research basis for positions the Commentary carries; the two are distinct artifacts within the same volume.[^2] The ZECNT series carries a three-register volume structure — Introduction, Commentary, Theology in Application — with each register articulated as a distinct artifact-stage.[^3] In each case, the published Commentary is downstream of a research stage that is partly carried into the publication and partly held behind it.

[^1]: The pattern is general across the evangelical commentary tradition; specific articulations vary by series. See the BECNT and ZECNT engagements below for two principled instances.

[^2]: Robert W. Yarbrough, series preface to *Baker Exegetical Commentary on the New Testament*, in any BECNT volume, e.g., D. A. Carson, *Matthew*, BECNT (Grand Rapids: Baker Academic, 2017), ix–xi. The series-preface articulates the research-anchored-Introduction / published-Commentary structure as a deliberate two-stage architecture serving the scholar-pastor audience. Engagement-layer: primary-text Tier 1 at the series-preface register.

[^3]: Clinton E. Arnold, series preface to *Zondervan Exegetical Commentary on the New Testament*, in any ZECNT volume, e.g., Edward W. Klink III, *John*, ZECNT (Grand Rapids: Zondervan, 2016), 11–14. The series-preface articulates the three-register volume structure as serving distinct pastoral-pedagogical purposes. Engagement-layer: primary-text Tier 1 at the series-preface register.

SECNT collapses this discontinuity. The Brief is not a research stage that produces the published Commentary as a downstream artifact; the Brief *is* the published Commentary at the manuscript register. The substantive argument the Brief articulates is the substantive argument the Commentary publishes. The apparatus the Brief carries in footnotes is the apparatus the Commentary publishes in footnotes. The sub-section structure the Brief carries is the page tree the Commentary publishes as. The research-to-publication transition is a register-shift within the same artifact — manuscript to web — rather than a discontinuity between artifacts.

The discipline operates at two registers. At the manuscript register, it constrains the Brief drafting workflow: what enters the Brief is what publishes; the apparatus moves from research-internal (ARB IDs, Block IDs, F-codes, locus numbering, confidence splits) to footnote register (source citations, engagement-layer disclosures, page anchors) during Brief drafting rather than at a publication-stage transformation.[^4] At the publication register, it constrains the page tree's substantive shape: the Commentary's page tree is the Brief's sub-section structure rendered for web reading, not a separate publication-stage architecture designed after the manuscript is complete. The reader of the published Commentary is reading what the scholarly manuscript articulates; the page tree the reader navigates is the manuscript's substantive structure.

[^4]: For the means-of-production articulation of how the apparatus-shift operates during Brief drafting under the matured ARB workflow, see Methodology.

This discipline grounds the apparatus-substantial register the published Commentary operates at. Because what is in the substantive artifact is what is published, the apparatus the Brief carries in footnotes — multi-source primary-text engagement at the load-bearing loci; engagement-layer disclosures at each source; page anchors at each citation — is the apparatus the published Commentary carries. The reader of the published Commentary engages the scholarly apparatus the manuscript articulates, not a publication-stage abridgment.

## §2 The audience-stratified dual-artifact landscape

The Commentary and the Lectures are coordinate artifacts serving coordinate audiences. The Commentary's primary audience is scholars, professors, and cross-disciplinary researchers engaging the substantive scholarly argument at apparatus-substantial register. The Lecture's primary audience is pastors, Bible study teachers, and ministers engaging the same substantive argument at the pedagogically-scaffolded register, where the listening reader cannot pause to look up a footnote and where pedagogical pacing carries the argument the apparatus elsewhere supports.

The seminary student is the third audience the architecture serves — typically engaging both artifacts, often layered. A common pattern: the Lecture supplies the overview and the pedagogical scaffolding; the Commentary supplies the apparatus depth and the source-engagement; the Lecture is returned to later as a teaching exemplar for the student's own subsequent ministry. The architecture serves three audiences with two artifacts, with each artifact operating at its register against the same substantive scholarly work.

This dual-artifact audience architecture differs from how surrounding evangelical commentary series stratify their audiences. The conventional pattern is one substantial artifact (the published commentary volume) targeting one primary audience, with secondary audiences engaging the same artifact at the registers it accommodates. A series may target scholars (NIGTC, NICNT), pastors (NIVAC, The Pillar New Testament Commentary at its application-oriented register, ZECNT), or exegesis students (EGGNT) — but the substantive artifact is one volume operating at one primary register, with the surrounding audiences inferring what they need from the registers the volume accommodates.

The audience-stratified dual-artifact landscape operates differently. Rather than one artifact accommodating multiple audiences at registers it can flex toward, SECNT publishes two artifacts at distinct registers serving distinct audiences against the same substantive scholarly work. The scholar reading the Commentary finds the apparatus at full depth — engagement-layer disclosure at every source, multi-tradition Tier 1 convergence at load-bearing loci, explicit confidence with "what could shift this" articulation, primary-text engagement at the load-bearing register. The pastor watching the Lecture finds the substantive argument at the pedagogically-scaffolded register — opposing views opened in their strongest form, pedagogical pacing with definitions and "let me restate" transitions, restatement of complex moves for the listening reader, engagement with the source material at the engagement-layer disclosed in the Commentary but without the footnote apparatus the listening register cannot carry.

The same substantive scholarly argument operates at both artifacts. The Lecture is not a popularization of the Commentary; the Commentary is not a footnote-laden version of the Lecture. The substantive argument — what the text means, what the project locks against it, what the project's confidence is and what could shift it — is what is shared between the artifacts. What differs is the register at which the argument operates, which is what each audience needs to engage it well.

This audience architecture grounds the next principle: how the substantive argument's shared register and the artifact-distinct registers (apparatus on the Commentary side, pedagogical scaffolding on the Lecture side) operationalize at the actual artifacts.[^5]

[^5]: For the means-of-production register at which the substantive argument is produced and rendered into the shared-and-distinct registers of the two artifacts, see Methodology.

## §3 The apparatus-substantial Commentary / pedagogically-scaffolded Lecture discipline

The artifact-and-audience-coordination principle that operationalizes §2's audience architecture: the **substantive argument is shared between the Commentary and the Lecture**; the **apparatus is Commentary-only**; the **pedagogical scaffolding is Lecture-only**. Each artifact carries what its audience needs to engage the substantive argument at its register.

The substantive argument — for and against the principal positions; the project's adopted position with the substantive grounds for the adoption; the explicit confidence and what could shift it — operates at both artifacts. A pastor watching the Lecture engages the same substantive argument a scholar reading the Commentary engages; what differs is not the argument but the rendering register.

The apparatus — multi-source primary-text engagement at the load-bearing loci, engagement-layer disclosures at every source, page anchors at every citation, footnote-register source citations — operates only at the Commentary. The Commentary's footnotes carry the apparatus that the audience engaging at apparatus-substantial register requires. The Lecture, operating in a register the listening reader cannot pause to look up footnotes from, does not carry the apparatus in audible form. The Lecture engages source material at the engagement-layer the Commentary discloses but does so at the substantive-argument register without surfacing the footnote machinery itself.

The pedagogical scaffolding — restated transitions, pedagogical pacing, definition and example interpolations, "let me restate" moves, summary anchors — operates only at the Lecture. The Lecture's pedagogical register expands the Commentary's prose by roughly 2.5× to 4× at the spoken-word register, where the expansion serves the listening reader who cannot re-read. The Commentary, operating in a register the reading scholar can re-read at need, does not carry the pedagogical scaffolding in extended form. The Commentary's prose at the substantive-argument register is what the scholar engages; the Lecture's scaffolded prose at the substantive-argument register is what the pastor engages.

The discipline operates through a structural separator at the Brief drafting stage: **footnotes are the structural separator between body text and apparatus**. The Brief's body text carries the substantive argument that both artifacts inherit. The Brief's footnotes carry the apparatus that only the Commentary publishes. The Lecture script expansion works from body-text material only; footnotes are the Commentary-side substrate the Lecture does not engage at the audible register. Word-count math for lecture runtime estimation operates against body-text word counts, not footnote-inclusive counts.

This discipline differs from how surrounding evangelical commentary series handle the audience-and-artifact relationship. The conventional pattern is one artifact operating at one register, with audiences who need a different register seeking it elsewhere — pastors seeking a separate pastoral commentary, popular treatments, or sermon-preparation resources; scholars seeking the more technical series at the same passage. The series do not coordinate the audience-and-artifact landscape; each series operates at its own register and the reader sorts among series to find what they need.

The Word Biblical Commentary articulates this conventional pattern explicitly at its overview, organizing each pericope into Bibliography, Translation, Notes, Form/Structure/Setting, Comment, and Explanation sections — six registers operating at one artifact, with the audience engaging whichever registers serve them.[^6] The audience is implicitly stratified by which registers each reader engages most; the artifact is one volume operating at all registers.

[^6]: David A. Hubbard and Glenn W. Barker, series preface to *Word Biblical Commentary*, in any WBC volume. The series-preface articulates the multi-register single-artifact structure as the WBC distinctive. Engagement-layer: primary-text Tier 1 at the series-preface register.

SECNT operates differently. The audience-stratified dual-artifact landscape is articulated at the series level: two artifacts at coordinate registers serving coordinate audiences against the same substantive scholarly work. The scholar does not sort within the volume to find the apparatus; the apparatus is the Commentary's substantive register. The pastor does not sort within the volume to find pedagogically-scaffolded engagement; the pedagogically-scaffolded register is the Lecture's substantive register. Each audience finds the artifact operating at its register, with the same substantive scholarly argument running through both.

What this discipline together with the Brief–Commentary identity discipline (§1) produces operationally: a single substantive scholarly manuscript (the Brief) carrying the substantive argument in body text and the apparatus in footnotes; the manuscript publishing as the Commentary at the web register with both body text and footnotes intact; the body text alone supplying the substantive argument the Lecture script expands at the pedagogical register. One scholarly manuscript; two coordinate published artifacts; three audiences served at the registers each requires.[^7]

[^7]: For the means-of-production articulation of how the Brief's footnote-structural-separator discipline operates during drafting and how the Lecture script expansion works from body-text material, see Methodology.

## §4 The reader-facing architectural diagrams discipline

The published Commentary carries visual representation of architectural content at substantive joints where visual representation clarifies the architectural argument. The diagrams are reader-facing artifacts at the published-Commentary visual register — designed for the reader engaging the substantive argument, not for the project's internal construction.

The discipline operates at three substantive commitments. **First**, diagrams are visual enhancement at substantive joints, not load-bearing scholarly content. The substantive argument is carried by the prose; the prose stands alone without the diagram. The diagram clarifies the architectural relationship the prose articulates; a reader who reads only the prose and does not engage the diagram does not miss the substantive argument. **Second**, diagrams render substantive content through structure and labels rather than through color or decorative elements. The diagrams render correctly in grayscale and at print register without loss of substantive content; the architectural relationships are carried by spatial arrangement, labels, and structural marks rather than by color-coded keys or visual ornament. **Third**, the published Commentary carries at most one diagram per page. The reader is not asked to integrate multiple visual representations within a single substantive sub-section; visual density is held to what the substantive argument requires at the specific joint.

These commitments distinguish the reader-facing diagrams from the project's internal Architecture Map, which operates as a research-side construction artifact at the project-internal register. The Architecture Map carries Block IDs, F-codes, coupling-pair markers, framework references, Brief Landing Matrix coordinates, and Update Log entries — apparatus the research workflow requires and the reader-facing audience does not need. The reader-facing diagrams strip the internal apparatus and carry the substantive architecture at the published-Commentary register: a diagram showing the foyer-bracket pattern in the Prologue depicts the v. 1 / v. 18 bracket-pair and the architectural function of the bracket without surfacing the Block IDs or framework references that underlie the locked argument behind it.

The discipline also articulates what diagrams substantively belong. A diagram belongs at a substantive joint where the architectural relationship is clarified by visual representation more than by prose alone — the Prologue's three-movement structure with the foyer-bracket arcing across; the macro-tense spine running diachronically through the unit; the coordinate articulation of two structural-architectural patterns operating simultaneously at distinct registers. A diagram does not belong at a substantive joint where prose carries the argument fully — clause-level grammatical analysis, lexical engagement, source-engagement reasoning. The discipline guards against decorative or illustrative use of visual content; every reader-facing diagram is at a substantive joint where the prose's architectural argument substantively benefits from visual representation.

The substantive specifications for which diagrams belong, where each lives, and what each diagram's substantive function is are articulated at the unit level at the Cycle Plan; the rendering register (typography, color palette, grayscale-safe design, SVG format, print-stylesheet integration) is articulated at the Visual Register.[^8] The discipline at the architectural register is what the diagrams substantively are; the unit-level specifications are what diagrams a specific unit carries; the Visual Register is how the diagrams render.

[^8]: For unit-specific diagram specifications, see the Cycle Plan §III.5 for the Jn 1:1–3 unit (and the corresponding Cycle Plan section for each subsequent unit). For the rendering register (typography, color, SVG format, grayscale-safety, print-stylesheet integration), see the SECNT Visual Register.

This discipline coordinates with the Brief–Commentary identity discipline (§1). The reader-facing diagrams are substantive content in the published Commentary — they belong at the unit-level page-tree structure the Brief's substantive structure determines. A diagram at the unit landing page is research-side specified; a diagram within a unit's substantive sub-section is research-side specified at that sub-section's joint. Production-side renders the diagrams from the research-side specifications. The diagrams are not auxiliary visual content added at publication; they are substantive content the Commentary publishes alongside its prose, specified at the same research-side discipline that produces the Brief.

## §5 The Architectural-Systematic Commentary Genre

The four principles together constitute a commentary genre this series is committed to: **architectural-systematic** at the seminary-pastoral register. The genre is not the sum of four independent commitments but the integrated substantive shape the four produce when they operate together. This closing synthesis articulates what the genre substantively is, what it produces for the reader who engages it, and what distinguishes it from the surrounding evangelical commentary genres at the substantive level.

### What the four principles together produce

The Brief–Commentary identity discipline (§1) commits that what the scholarly manuscript articulates is what the published Commentary carries. The audience-stratified dual-artifact landscape (§2) commits that scholars and pastors are coordinate audiences served by coordinate artifacts. The apparatus-substantial Commentary / pedagogically-scaffolded Lecture discipline (§3) commits that the substantive scholarly argument operates at both artifacts at registers each audience requires. The reader-facing architectural diagrams discipline (§4) commits that visual representation serves the substantive argument at compositional joints where prose alone leaves the architecture underspecified.

When these four operate together, what they produce is a commentary that does substantively what its surrounding tradition does not: it carries **the compositional architecture of the biblical text as substantive content of the published Commentary**, rendered at apparatus-substantial register for the scholar and at pedagogically-scaffolded register for the pastor, with reader-facing visual representation at the architectural joints where the prose's compositional argument substantively benefits from it. The architecture is not auxiliary observation about the text; it is part of what the commentary substantively engages and what the reader substantively learns.

This is what makes the genre architectural-systematic. **Architectural**: the published Commentary carries deliberate-compositional articulation of the biblical text's structural shape at the unit-wide register, not only clause-level exegesis. **Systematic**: the architectural articulation operates at multiple registers (synchronic shape; diachronic spine; coordinate articulation of shape and spine; integrative loci) and the registers cohere as one underlying compositional architecture rather than as alternatives or duplicates. The genre's distinctive contribution is articulating the biblical text's compositional architecture substantively as content the reader engages, not as scholar's-eye-only observation behind the text the commentary publishes.

### The unit-wide architectural content

At the John 1 unit, two compositional patterns operate at the Prologue's deliberate-compositional register. **The foyer-bracket pattern** at v. 1 / v. 18 — the Word who is *πρὸς τὸν θεόν* at v. 1 and the Son who is *εἰς τὸν κόλπον τοῦ πατρός* at v. 18 — operates as the Prologue's outer architectural bracket, with the substantive content of "personal fellowship with God" carried through the bracket-pair as the Prologue's synchronic frame. **The macro-tense spine** running diachronically through the Prologue — *ἦν* (timeless-eternal) at vv. 1–4; *ἐγένετο* (historical-creational and historical-incarnational) at the moments where the text moves from eternal to temporal; *ὃ γέγονεν* (resulting-state) at the perfect tenses where the historical action persists into its enduring consequence — operates as the Prologue's diachronic axis, with the substantive content of "the eternal Word's movement through historical creation to incarnation and resulting reality" carried through the macro-tense pattern.

These two patterns are not interpretive overlays on the text; they are substantively present at the text's compositional shape. The foyer-bracket is what the same author's word choice — *κόλπος* at v. 18 echoing *πρός* at v. 1 — does at the deliberate-compositional register. The macro-tense spine is what the same author's tense choice — *ἦν* for eternal being, *ἐγένετο* for historical occurrence, *γέγονεν* for resulting state — does across the Prologue's eighteen verses. The patterns are author's compositional craft at the unit-wide register; the commentary substantively engaging the text engages this craft as substantive content.

What makes the unit-wide articulation substantively load-bearing rather than ornamental observation is the **multi-tradition convergence at primary-text register** the patterns rest on. The foyer-bracket pattern rests on five independent commentary traditions converging on the v. 1 / v. 18 architectural relationship at primary-text register (Schnackenburg; McHugh; Brown; Barrett; Westcott). The macro-tense spine rests on seven independent commentary traditions converging on the *ἦν / ἐγένετο* compositional pattern at primary-text register (Ridderbos; Zerwick-Grosvenor; McHugh; Brown; Carson; Schnackenburg; with the project's three-term *ἦν / ἐγένετο / ὃ γέγονεν* articulation operating downstream of the two-term reference-grammar framing). When multiple independent traditions converge at primary-text register on the same substantive observation, the observation is substantively present at the text rather than projected onto it. The unit-wide architectural content the published Commentary carries is content multiple traditions have substantively engaged; the commentary's contribution is articulating it at the integrated substantive register the surrounding tradition operates at fragmentarily.[^9]

### The coordinate-register lock

The published Commentary carries locked positions at coordinate registers. A locked position at a specific locus — what the imperfect *ἦν* at John 1:1a substantively predicates of the Word; what the perfect *γέγονεν* at John 1:3c–4a substantively produces at the v. 3 → v. 4 boundary — operates at the **locus-level register** where the clause-level substantive resolution carries. The same locked position also operates at the **architectural-thesis register** where the locus-level resolution grounds the unit-wide compositional pattern's substantive shape. *Γέγονεν* at the v. 3 → v. 4 pivot operates locus-level as the perfect-tense resulting-state predication of created reality; it operates architectural-thesis register as the substantive ground for the project's CreationalPivot articulation at the v. 3 / v. 4 boundary.

The coordinate-register lock means a reader engaging the published Commentary at one locus engages substantive content that also operates at the unit-wide compositional pattern, with both registers articulated explicitly. The reader is not asked to integrate the locus-level resolution into the unit-wide architecture interpretively; the commentary articulates the coordinate registers substantively. This is what apparatus-substantial register substantively produces — locked positions at the register where they substantively operate, with the registers and the grounds for each register articulated transparently.[^10]

[^10]: For the means-of-production articulation of how the dual-register lock workflow produces locked positions at coordinate registers during the ARB drafting cycle, see Methodology.

### Cross-references as structural dependencies

Because the commentary engages the biblical text's compositional architecture as substantive content, cross-references in the published Commentary operate as **structural dependencies** rather than thematic associations. A cross-reference from John 1:3 to Genesis 1:1 operates at the architectural register because the *ἐγένετο* at John 1:3 substantively engages the Genesis 1 creation account at the source-text relationship the Fourth Gospel's compositional register establishes — not because both passages thematically concern creation. A cross-reference from John 1:4 to Revelation 21–22 operates at the architectural register because the eschatological light-and-life Logic of Revelation 21–22 substantively engages the Prologue's *zōē / phōs* articulation at the canonical relationship the Fourth Gospel's compositional register opens — not because both passages thematically concern light.

Cross-references operate as substantive structural dependencies in the architectural-systematic genre because the genre engages the text's compositional architecture substantively. Where the compositional architecture establishes a substantive relationship between texts, cross-reference is structural; where the compositional architecture does not establish such a relationship, cross-reference is held back even if the texts thematically associate. This discipline guards the reader from cross-reference inflation that obscures the substantive architectural relationships the commentary articulates.[^11]

### What the genre is, distinct from its surrounding tradition

The architectural-systematic commentary genre operates at a register the surrounding evangelical commentary tradition does not articulate as the genre's distinctive substantive contribution.

The exegetical commentary genre (BECNT, NICNT) operates at the clause-level register at full primary-text depth, with architectural observations about the text's compositional shape often present at the volume's Introduction or at unit-introduction sub-sections, but not articulated as the substantive content the published Commentary carries verse-by-verse. The architecture lives at the Introduction; the Commentary lives at the clauses.

The pastoral commentary genre (NIVAC, ZECNT's Theology in Application sections) operates at the pastoral-application register where the text's substantive content is rendered for ministry contexts, with architectural observations about the compositional shape typically held at the introductory or theological-application register rather than at the substantive content the commentary verse-by-verse carries.

The grammatical commentary genre (NIGTC, EGGNT, Wallace's grammars at the reference register) operates at the clause-level grammatical analysis register at full technical depth, with architectural observations typically operating at the level of paragraph-or-pericope structure rather than at the deliberate-compositional register the unit-wide patterns substantively occupy.

The critical-historical commentary genre (Hermeneia, Anchor Bible) operates at the historical-critical register where source criticism, historical context, and reception-history substantively engage the text, with architectural observations sometimes present at the unit-wide register (especially in the case of Brown's Anchor John on the Prologue) but operating as observations on the text rather than as the genre's distinctive substantive contribution to the reader's engagement.

The architectural-systematic commentary genre articulates what these surrounding genres articulate fragmentarily — the biblical text's compositional architecture — as the genre's distinctive substantive contribution at the verse-by-verse register, with multi-tradition primary-text grounding making the architectural articulation substantively load-bearing, with coordinate-register locks at the loci where the unit-wide architecture rests, and with cross-references operating as structural dependencies serving the architectural articulation. The reader engaging the architectural-systematic commentary substantively engages the text's compositional craft as part of what the commentary substantively teaches — not as scholar's-eye-only observation behind the text the reader is given, but as substantive content the reader is taught alongside the text the reader reads.

This is the genre this series is committed to. The four principles articulated above produce it operationally; the substantive purpose it serves is the reader's substantive engagement with what the biblical text substantively articulates at every register the text operates at — including the compositional register where the Fourth Evangelist's deliberate craft substantively carries the substantive content the Prologue teaches.
