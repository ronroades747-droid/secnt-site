# Citing SECNT

The series is freely available under a Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International license (CC BY 4.0). You are free to quote it, share it, and build on it — in teaching, in publication, in translation, for any purpose — provided you give appropriate credit and indicate any changes you make. This page sets out how to cite the Commentary and the Lectures, and what the license asks in return.

## What the license asks

Attribution under CC BY 4.0 means naming the work and its editor, indicating the license, and linking back where it is practical to do so. Reuse and adaptation are permitted — including commercial use and derivative works — so long as attribution is given and any modifications are indicated. The full terms are at <https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/>.

## Citing the Commentary

Because the published Commentary is a living text that may be refined over time (see [Methodology](/methodology/) on how positions are refined), cite the passage you used and the date you accessed it.

**Full form:**

> Roades, Ronald E., ed. *Commentary on the Gospel of John*, on [passage]. Systematic Evangelical Commentaries on the New Testament. [stable URL]. Accessed [date].

**Short form:**

> Roades, ed., *SECNT: John*, on [passage] (accessed [date]).

## Citing the Lectures

Each Lecture teaches a passage of the Commentary and may be cited on its own.

**Full form:**

> Roades, Ronald E., ed. "[Lecture title]." Lecture on [passage]. *Commentary on the Gospel of John*, Systematic Evangelical Commentaries on the New Testament. [stable URL]. Accessed [date].

## Stable references

The series is built to last, and its addresses are meant to be permanent. Cite by passage and by the stable URL, and the reference will resolve; where a section is later refined, the access date marks the form you used.

## Contact

For questions about citing, reuse, or permissions beyond what the license already grants, write to <editor@secnt.org>.
